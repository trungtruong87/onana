How to Use This Guide

This guide uses simple English. Every step tells you exactly what to do. Read each step fully before doing it. Never skip a step. If a step says STOP and check — stop and check.

✅ CHECK — Confirm the step worked before moving on

🎒 CARRY — Everything you need to bring to that room

PART 2 — ON FIELD: Install devices room by room at the warehouse.

What Each Device Does

📦 Meraki AR112 — Wall Rack Cabinet
Metal cabinet that bolts to the wall in Closet 104. All rack devices live inside this.
📍 Wall of Closet 104
🔋 APC Smart-UPS SRT 2200VA — Battery Backup
If the building loses power, this keeps all devices running 15–25 minutes. Without it, a power cut kills everything instantly.
📍 Inside rack cabinet
🌐 Meraki MX68 — Router (Internet Gateway)
The front door to the internet. Connects to your ISP (AT&T fiber or Spectrum cable) and manages the 5G backup. Also creates and separates network zones (VLANs) — including main Wi-Fi (AD login) and guest Wi-Fi (password).
📍 Inside rack cabinet
🔀 Meraki MS125-48FP — Switch (Device Hub)
Connects all devices together. Sends power through the data cable (PoE) so cameras and APs don't need separate power plugs at each location.
📍 Inside rack cabinet
📶 T-Mobile MG21 — 5G Backup Internet
Backup internet using a SIM card. When the main ISP goes down, this takes over automatically within 40 seconds.
📍 Inside rack cabinet
📡 Meraki MR57 ×6 — Ceiling Wi-Fi Access Points
Round white discs that mount on the ceiling. They broadcast two networks: CORP-OFFICE (staff, AD login) and GUEST (visitor, password only).
📍 Ceilings throughout building

📡 ISP Connection — AT&T Fiber OR Spectrum Cable

The MX68 router supports both fiber and cable. Use the correct diagram for your ISP.

Option A — AT&T Business Fiber

AT&T Fiber Line
→
AT&T ONT (fiber converter box)
Wall-mounted, installed by AT&T
→
CAT6 cable from ONT LAN port
→
MX68 WAN1 port

AT&T installs the ONT on the wall near the building's fiber entry point. They give you one Ethernet port (LAN) out of the ONT. Run a CAT6 cable from that port to the MX68's WAN1 port. That is the only cable you need from AT&T.

⚠️

Do NOT connect anything to the AT&T ONT's WAN/GPON port — that is the fiber line. Only use the LAN Ethernet port.

Option B — Spectrum Business Cable

Spectrum Coax Line
→
Spectrum Cable Modem
Installed by Spectrum or you rent/buy
→
CAT6 cable from modem LAN port
→
MX68 WAN1 port

Spectrum runs a coax (TV-style round cable) to the building. The cable modem converts it to Ethernet. Run a CAT6 cable from the modem's LAN/Ethernet port into the MX68's WAN1 port.

Modem in bridge mode (Spectrum only)

Call Spectrum and ask them to put the modem in bridge mode. This turns off the modem's own router, so only the MX68 manages the network. If the modem stays in router mode, you get double-NAT which causes connection problems.

Full Network Connection Diagram — Closet 104 Rack

Every device in the rack. Read top to bottom — this is the order you also power things on.

[UPS] — plugged into 20A wall outlet
  ↓ powers everything below via PDU
[PDU 1U power strip] — plugged into UPS output
  ↓
[MX68 Router]
  ↑ WAN1 port ← CAT6 from AT&T ONT or Spectrum modem
  ↓ LAN port → CAT6 → Switch port 47

PoE = Power over Ethernet

The switch sends both data AND power through the CAT6 cable. When you plug a cable from the switch into an MR57 AP, it powers on immediately — no separate power adapter needed at each AP or the MG21.

PART 1 — DRY RUN

Test all devices at home (or any desk with internet) before going to the warehouse. Fix problems now — it is much easier before anything is mounted permanently.

Rule: Test first, install second.

Never mount a device permanently until it shows "Online" in the Meraki Dashboard. This one rule saves hours of frustration.

DR-0 — Preconditions | Do These Before Touching Any Hardware
DR-0.1Confirm Meraki Dashboard Access▼
  • 1
    Connect your laptop to any working Wi-Fi or phone hotspot.
  • 2
    Open a browser. Go to dashboard.meraki.com. Log in with your credentials.
  • 3
    Confirm you can see the Dashboard home screen and your organisation's network.
✅

CHECK: Dashboard loads. You can see your network. If login fails, contact whoever gave you the credentials before continuing.

DR-0.2Claim All Devices to the Dashboard▼
What "claiming" means:

Each Meraki device has a serial number. You type that serial number into the Dashboard to link the physical device to your network account. Without this, the device won't register.

  • 1
    In the Dashboard, navigate to Organization → Inventory.
  • 2
    Click Claim and enter the serial number for each device: MS125-48FP switch, MX68 router, MG21, and all 6 MR57 APs. The serial number is a sticker on the back or bottom of each device.
  • 3
    Assign each device to the correct network. Name them clearly (e.g. "AP-Lobby", "AP-Warehouse-West").
✅

CHECK: All 9 devices appear in Inventory. Each is assigned to a network.

DR-0.3Configure Wi-Fi Networks (SSIDs)▼
Two Wi-Fi networks you need to create:

CORP-OFFICE (main staff network) — uses Active Directory (AD) login. Staff enter their company username and password to connect. This keeps the network secure and uses existing company accounts.

GUEST — visitor network. Uses a simple shared password. Guests are blocked from the staff network (VLAN isolation).

  • 1
    In Dashboard, go to Wireless → SSIDs.
  • 2
    Create SSID #1: Name = CORP-OFFICE. Security = WPA2-Enterprise / 802.1x. In the RADIUS settings, enter your company's Active Directory (AD) RADIUS server address, port (usually 1812), and shared secret. This forces staff to log in with their AD username and password.
  • 3
    Create SSID #2: Name = GUEST. Security = WPA2-Personal. Set a shared password (e.g., something easy for visitors to type). Enable Client Isolation so guests cannot reach the CORP-OFFICE network or internal servers.
  • 4
    Assign CORP-OFFICE to VLAN 10 and GUEST to VLAN 20. This is the invisible wall between the two networks.
⚠️

If your company does not yet have an AD RADIUS server, contact your IT admin before this step. CORP-OFFICE Wi-Fi will not work without it.

✅

CHECK: Both SSIDs appear in Dashboard. CORP-OFFICE set to WPA2-Enterprise. GUEST set to WPA2-Personal with client isolation enabled.

DR-1 — Home Table Setup
What you're doing:

You're setting up all rack devices temporarily on a table at home to test that everything powers on and registers in the Dashboard. The UPS is used here exactly as it will be at the warehouse — it is the power source for the MX68 and switch, not the wall outlet directly. The UPS plugs into the wall; everything else plugs into the UPS.

DR-1.1Connect ISP Router → MX68 WAN1 → Switch (see diagram)▼
The ISP router always connects to MX68 WAN1 — both at home and at the warehouse.

At home your existing internet router (any brand — Xfinity, Google Nest, etc.) stands in for the real ISP. Its LAN port plugs into the MX68's WAN1 port, exactly the same way AT&T or Spectrum will connect at the warehouse. The MX68 then connects to the switch via its LAN port. The switch distributes everything from there via PoE. IP addresses will look different at home (192.168.x.x instead of 10.0.10.x) — this is normal.

  • 1
    Place the UPS, MS125-48FP switch, and MX68 on your table.
  • 2
    Plug the UPS power cable into a wall outlet. Press the UPS power button. Wait 2 minutes until the front panel shows ON.
  • 3
    Plug the MX68 power adapter into one of the UPS output sockets. Wait 1 minute for the MX68 to boot.
  • 4
    Take a short CAT6 patch cable. Plug one end into a LAN port on the back of your home router. Plug the other end into the MX68 WAN1 port. This is the internet feed into the MX68.
  • 5
    Plug the MS125-48FP switch power cable into another UPS output socket. Wait 2 minutes — port LEDs will light up.
  • 6
    Take another short CAT6 cable. Plug one end into the MX68 LAN port (any of ports 1–4 on the back). Plug the other end into the switch SFP uplink port (or port 47). This feeds internet from the MX68 into the switch for all other devices.
✅

CHECK: UPS front panel shows ON. Switch LEDs lit. Dashboard shows MS125 and MX68 both Online within 5–10 minutes.

DR-1.2Connect MG21 and APs to the Switch (PoE)▼
PoE = Power over Ethernet.

The switch sends power through a CAT6 cable. The MG21 uses two cables: Port 1 receives PoE power from switch port 48, and Port 2 sends 5G internet data to the MX68 WAN2 port. The APs each use one cable that carries both power and data.

  • 1
    MG21 5G — power: Plug a CAT6 cable from switch port 48 into the MG21 Port 1 (PoE). The switch delivers power through this cable — no separate power adapter needed. No SIM yet — that is fine.
  • 2
    MG21 5G — failover uplink: Plug a CAT6 cable from the MG21 Port 2 (LAN) into the MX68 WAN2 port. This is what enables automatic failover — the MX68 routes internet through this cable to 5G if the ISP goes down.
  • 3
    MR57 AP #1: Plug a CAT6 cable from switch port 1 into the network port on the back of the first MR57. It powers on immediately through the cable.
  • 4
    MR57 AP #2: Plug a CAT6 cable from switch port 2 into the second MR57.
  • 5
    Wait 5–10 minutes for all devices to fully boot and register to the Meraki cloud.
✅

CHECK: MG21 and both MR57s powered on with no separate adapters. If a device doesn't power on, check the cable is firmly clicked at both ends.

DR-2 — Power On (In This Exact Order at Warehouse)
At home the order doesn't matter (no UPS). At the warehouse, follow this exact sequence.

The UPS powers everything. Power devices in the wrong order and you risk the switch coming online before the router — which causes the network to not form correctly.

DR-3.3Configure 5G Failover in Dashboard (Required for Automatic Switchover)▼
⚠️

This step is required. Without it, the MX68 will not know the MG21 is a backup uplink and will NOT automatically switch to 5G when Spectrum goes down. The physical cable alone is not enough — Dashboard must be configured.

  • 1
    In Dashboard, go to Security & SD-WAN → Appliance Uplinks.
  • 2
    Confirm WAN1 shows the ISP connection (it should already be active and green from DR-3.1).
  • 3
    Under WAN2, select the uplink type as Cellular / MG21. This tells the MX68 that its WAN2 port is connected to the 5G gateway.
  • 4
    Set the uplink mode to Failover (not Load Balance). Set WAN1 as primary. This means all traffic goes through Spectrum normally, and the MX68 only switches to MG21 5G if WAN1 fails.
  • 5
    Save the settings. Dashboard will show WAN2/MG21 as a standby uplink.
At home during dry run:

WAN2 will show as connected but the MG21 has no SIM yet so 5G won't be active — that is expected. The failover test (unplugging ISP and confirming 5G takes over within 40 seconds) is done at the warehouse after inserting the activated SIM. See DR-4.

✅

CHECK: Dashboard → Appliance Uplinks shows WAN1 = Primary (ISP), WAN2 = Standby (MG21/Cellular). Failover mode is set.

🎉 Home Dry Run Complete!

All checks passed = every device powers on and registers correctly. The VLANs, firewall rules, and SSIDs you configured are saved in the cloud. When MX68 powers on at the warehouse with the real ISP connected, it automatically downloads and activates those settings.

PART 2 — ON FIELD

Install devices room by room. Work one room at a time. Never close a ceiling or seal a wall until every cable in that area passes the cable tester.

OF-0 — Cabling Rules | Read Before Pulling Any Cable
WhereHow to Run the CableWhy
Office rooms
(false ceiling with removable tiles)		Push the ceiling tile up and slide it aside. Feed the cable through the space above tiles. Use J-hooks to hold the cable up every 4–5 feet.No drilling needed — the space above tiles is hollow and open.
Warehouse

⚠️

Wire colour order — T568B — use at BOTH ends of every cable

PinColourPinColour
1White / Orange5White / Blue
2Orange6Green
3White / Green7White / Brown
4Blue8Brown
⚠️

Both ends of every cable MUST use T568B. One wrong end = cable fails tester = device won't work.

How to use the cable tester

Plug one end of the cable into the main tester unit and the other end into the remote unit. Turn it on. Each pin (1–8) lights up in sequence. All 8 lights on both units = PASS. Any light missing or wrong order = FAIL — re-punch that end and test again.

🔧 Troubleshooting
ProblemFix
Device shows Offline in Dashboard1) Check patch cable at both ends — click firmly. 2) Confirm switch port LED is lit. 3) Confirm the switch itself is Online. 4) Reboot the device by unplugging and re-plugging the cable.
AP powers on but stays offline > 10 minCheck the switch port has PoE enabled in Dashboard → Switching → Switch Ports. Also confirm the cable test passed all 8 pins.
CORP-OFFICE Wi-Fi asks for username/password but failsConfirm RADIUS server is reachable from the MX68. Check the RADIUS secret in Dashboard → Wireless → SSIDs matches what the AD server expects. Test with a known-good AD account.
GUEST Wi-Fi connects but no internetCheck VLAN 20 is configured on the MX68 with a DHCP scope. Confirm the GUEST SSID is assigned to VLAN 20.
No internet at all — ISP downConfirm MX68 WAN1 shows connected in Dashboard. Check ISP equipment (ONT or modem) has power and normal LEDs. Call ISP support.
5G failover doesn't switch1) Confirm SIM is activated and inserted correctly in MG21. 2) Check the CAT6 cable from switch port 48 to MG21 Port 1 (PoE power) is firmly seated. 3) Check the CAT6 cable from MG21 Port 2 to MX68 WAN2 is firmly seated. 4) Check Dashboard → Security & SD-WAN → Appliance Uplinks — WAN2 must be set to Cellular/MG21 in Failover mode with WAN1 as primary (see DR-3.3).
Cable fails testerOne or more wires are in wrong order. Unclip the connector, re-punch in T568B order, re-test. Never re-use a damaged connector — cut it off and use a fresh one.
